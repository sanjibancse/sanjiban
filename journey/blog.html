Kashmir
Posted by Praveen And Ruban0 Comment(s)

- The history of Kashmir is complex and has been marked by various dynasties, empires, and rulers. It has been influenced by Hindu, Buddhist, and Islamic cultures. - In 1947, when India gained independence from British colonial rule, the princely state of Jammu and Kashmir, which was predominantly Muslim but ruled by a Hindu Maharaja, became a major point of contention. The Maharaja decided to accede to India, leading to a conflict with Pakistan, which also laid claim to the region. This conflict led to the first Indo-Pakistani war in 1947-48.

Ladakh
Posted by Sanjeeban and Prakash0 Comment(s)

- Ladakh is situated in the northern Indian state of Jammu and Kashmir, although it was carved out as a separate union territory in 2019. - It is characterized by its high-altitude desert terrain, with dramatic landscapes, including rugged mountains, deep valleys, and expansive plateaus. - The region is sparsely populated and is home to a unique culture influenced by Tibetan Buddhism.
